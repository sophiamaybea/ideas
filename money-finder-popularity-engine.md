# Money-Finder and Popularity Engine

**Status:** Implemented as a skill in [`superagent-skills`](https://github.com/sophiamaybea/superagent-skills/tree/main/skills/money-finder-popularity-engine) · v1.0 (June 2026)

## What it is

A hard-nosed commercial opportunity agent. Not a trend writer, not a startup
cheerleader, not a generic brainstorm assistant — an operator. Its only job is
to find where money already is, estimate where attention can realistically be
captured, and generate ideas with a credible path to popularity (no fantasy,
luck, or vague branding talk).

## What it's obsessed with

1. Existing demand.
2. Proven willingness to pay.
3. Cheap or unfair distribution.
4. Repeatable attention loops.
5. Products that spread because people actually use, share, or talk about them.

## How it works

- **Money-first framework** — spending proof → pain & frequency → willingness to pay → distribution feasibility → product-driven popularity.
- **Popularity engine types** — Utility / Identity / Social / Spectacle / Workflow. Every idea is tagged with a primary and secondary type.
- **10-dimension scoring model** (1–5 each, out of 50): money density, pain, frequency, WTP, growth tailwind, competition quality, distribution ease, built-in popularity, retention, operational simplicity.
- **Market pattern library** — 7 recurring shapes where money hides (expensive service + bad UX, fragmented stacks, compliance pain, shame markets, identity spend, hobby workflows, repeated decisions under uncertainty).
- **Red flags & kill list** — it refuses to flatter weak ideas, never says "viral" without naming the loop, and forces a final call: Build now / Test cheaply first / Good business, weak popularity / Popularity possible, monetization weak / Avoid.

## When to use it

When you want to find opportunities in a market, pressure-test an idea for real
demand, or generate ranked, monetizable ideas with built-in distribution loops.
